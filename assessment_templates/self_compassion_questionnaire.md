---

## Understanding the Six Subscales

Self-compassion involves **three positive dimensions** (higher scores = more self-compassion):
- **Self-Kindness (SK):** Being warm and understanding toward yourself
- **Common Humanity (CH):** Recognizing suffering as part of shared human experience
- **Mindfulness (M):** Holding painful thoughts/feelings in balanced awareness

And **three negative dimensions** (lower scores = more self-compassion):
- **Self-Judgment (SJ):** Being harsh and critical toward yourself
- **Isolation (I):** Feeling alone and separate in your suffering
- **Over-Identification (OI):** Getting swept up in painful thoughts/feelings

## Interpretation Guide

### Overall Score Ranges

| Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| **1.0 - 2.5** | Low self-compassion. Would benefit significantly from learning to be more supportive toward yourself. |
| **2.5 - 3.5** | Moderate self-compassion. Could practice it more consistently in your life. Average is ~3.0. |
| **3.5 - 5.0** | High self-compassion. You are often a good friend to yourself. |

### Subscale Interpretation

**Positive Subscales (SK, CH, M):**
- Score < 3.0 = Area to strengthen
- Score ≥ 3.0 = Adequate to strong

**Negative Subscales (SJ, I, OI):**
- Score > 3.0 = Area to address (reduce this pattern)
- Score ≤ 3.0 = Adequate to healthy
