### STRONGEST SKILLS (Top 3)

#### 1. Goal-Directed Persistence — 18/21 ⭐
*The capacity to have a goal, follow through to the completion of the goal, and not be put off by or distracted by competing interests.*

**Behaviors assessed:**

- I easily give up immediate pleasures to work on long-term goals

**Interpretation:** This is your standout strength. You know what you want and maintain drive toward goals. This can be leveraged to compensate for weaker skills by tying tasks to meaningful goals.

#### 2. Organization — 16/21

**Behaviors assessed:**
- I am an organized person
- It is natural for me to keep my work area neat and organized
- I am good at maintaining systems for organizing my work

**Interpretation:** You have a natural ability to create systems. This strength supports building the external scaffolding needed for challenge areas.

---

#### 3. Task Initiation — 14/21
*The ability to begin projects without undue procrastination, in an efficient or timely fashion.*

**Behaviors assessed:**
- No matter what the task, I believe in getting started as soon as possible
- Procrastination is usually not a problem for me
- I seldom leave tasks to the last minute

**Interpretation:** Moderate strength—you can get started when motivated. Combined with Goal-Directed Persistence, this suggests procrastination is less about starting and more about maintaining momentum.

### CHALLENGE AREAS (Bottom 3)

#### 12. Emotional Control — 3/21 ⚠️
*The ability to manage emotions in order to achieve goals, complete tasks, or control and direct behavior.*

**Behaviors assessed:**
- My emotions seldom get in the way when performing on the job
- Little things do not affect me emotionally or distract me from the task at hand
- I can defer my personal feelings until after a task has been completed

**Interpretation:** This is your most significant challenge area. Emotions frequently interfere with task completion and job performance. This aligns with:
- RSD (Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria) patterns identified in your Values Assessment
- The "slippery behavior" of becoming ego-based vs. curiosity-based
- Your 360 feedback noting emotional investment needing balance with business needs

**Scaffolding needed:**
- DBT skills (STOP, TIPP, Check the Facts)
- Pre-planned emotional regulation strategies
- Buffer time before responding to triggering situations

---

#### 11. Time Management — 6/21 ⚠️
*The capacity to estimate how much time one has, how to allocate it, and how to stay within time limits and deadlines.*

**Behaviors assessed:**
- At the end of the day, I've usually finished what I set out to do
- I am good at estimating how long it takes to do something
- I am usually on time for appointments and activities

**Interpretation:** Time blindness is a significant challenge. Difficulty estimating duration and staying within time limits. This is a classic ADHD presentation.

**Scaffolding needed:**
- External time tracking (timers, time-boxing)
- Overestimate time requirements by 2x
- Visual countdowns and deadline reminders
- Time anchors throughout the day

---

#### 10. Working Memory — 7/21 ⚠️
*The ability to hold information in memory while performing complex tasks.*

**Behaviors assessed:**
- I have a good memory for facts, dates, and details
- I am very good at remembering the things I have committed to do
- I seldom need reminders to complete tasks

**Interpretation:** Information doesn't stay "online" in working memory. This affects:
- Remembering commitments without external capture
- Holding multiple pieces of information while problem-solving
- Following through on intentions

**Scaffolding needed:**
- External capture systems (everything out of head immediately)
- Written/recorded reminders for all commitments
- Checklists for multi-step processes
- Single-tasking vs. multi-tasking
