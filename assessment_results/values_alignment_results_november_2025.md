# Values Alignment Results - November 2025

## Core Values Identified

### Value 1: Well-being
**Supporting Behaviors:**
- Striving for good sleep hygiene, daily exercise, daily meditation
- Knowing at my core that being healthy and feeling a sense of well-being is the foundation upon which the rest of my life rests—if I'm not healthy, I cannot continue to work on things that bring me purpose (learning) and I cannot show up for my loved ones (connection)

**Slippery Behaviors:**
- Emotional, addictive eating (particularly around sugar)
- Poor stress management

**Living Into Example:**
> Currently! Most intentional prioritization of well-being I think I've ever done.

---

### Value 2: Learning
**Supporting Behaviors:**
- Always going deep on the industry/products I'm working on professionally
- Staying curious across many different topics
- Trying to live every day prioritizing learning

**Slippery Behaviors:**
- When I let RSD and other ADHD or trauma-based anxieties dictate my behavior—becoming ego-based as opposed to curiosity-based in interactions with others
- Historically slipped mostly in professional settings, but also with parents on topics like politics where we differ in our POVs

**Living Into Example:**
> Currently! Most intentional prioritization of learning I think I've ever done—across the skills I need to manage myself with self-respect and consistency; and across upskilling that will help me professionally in my next role. I am also prioritizing hobby learning like starting tennis lessons.

---

### Value 3: Connection
**Supporting Behaviors:**
- Showing up for loved ones consistently
- Being present in conversations
- Vulnerability in close relationships

**Slippery Behaviors:**
- Isolating when stressed or overwhelmed
- Prioritizing work over relationships
- Surface-level interactions to avoid deeper engagement

**Living Into Example:**
> When I have social battery—showing up fully present for friends and family, making time for meaningful conversations even during busy periods.

## Key Insights

### The Foundation Insight
Well-being is foundational—without it, the other two values (Learning and Connection) cannot be sustained. This creates a hierarchy:
1. **Well-being** → enables
2. **Learning** → fuels purpose → creates energy for
3. **Connection** → sustained by the other two

### The ADHD-Values Intersection
The slippery behaviors for Learning (RSD, ego-based reactions) are directly connected to ADHD traits. This means:
- Values alignment requires managing ADHD symptoms
- External scaffolding is essential, not optional
- The "failure to follow through" pattern affects all three values

### Current State: Strong Alignment
Both Well-being and Learning show "currently living into this value" examples, indicating a period of intentional alignment. The social battery returning is evidence of system-wide improvement.
